Our Mother of Perpetual Help Redemptorist Church: Sunday Mass

   On Sunday, September 9, 2025, from 9:00 AM to 10:00 AM, I attended the Sunday Mass at Our Mother of Perpetual Help Redemptorist Church as an attendee. This church is my go-to place for worship every Sunday, so it already feels like a second home to me. The priests homily that day focused on the wrongdoing of politicians who committed ghost projects for flood money worth millions. He emphasizes the importance of honesty and integrity, especially for those in positions of power. Despite the power outage that occurred during the Mass, everyone stayed calm and continued to listen attentively, which made the message even more powerful.

   The priests words opened my eyes to how corruption and greed can deeply affect the live of ordinary people. I didnt expect such a direct and fearless message, but it reminded me that the Church can also be a voice for truth and justice. The power outage could have made the atmosphere more solemn and reflective. I admire how the priest spoke with courage, addressing a serious issue that may tend to ignore. The most satisfying part of the experience was feeling inspired to live with integrity and stand up for what is right, even when it is difficult.

   This Mass strengthened my belief that faith and social responsibility go hand in hand. I learned that being a good Catholic also means being aware of the injustices happening around us. I can apply this learning by being more discerning, honest, and vocal about what is right in my own community. I plan to continue supporting my church not just spiritually but also through active participation in activities that promote fairness and compassion. If I could experience that Mass again, I would take a moment to thank the priest for bravely speaking about the truth despite the circumstances.

   Because of this activity, I am more aware of the need to uphold truth and justice in all aspects of life. I am encouraged to live with integrity and to speak up when I see wrongdoing. Most of all, I am thankful for the reminder that faith should move us not just to pray, but also to act with courage and compassion for others.

University Christian Life Emphasis Month: God Cares

   On Thursday, October 9, 2025, from 5:00 PM to 7:00 PM, I attended an activity at Silliman University as an attendee. The objective of the session was to send a message that God cares, and it was a part of an activity for all first-year students. The event started at the Amphitheater, where we had an introduction and warm-up activity that brought everyones energy together. However, when the rain started to pour, we moved into the classrooms to continue the event. There, we began the yarn string activity, passing a string to people we wanted to know better, symbolizing a connection.

   I thought the activity would be more formal or serious, but it turned out to be meaningful and interactive. The yarn activity helped everyone relax and build friendships. We also reflected on 1 Kings 19:1-18, which taught us that Gods presence is often found in gentle and quiet moments rather than in loud or powerful ones. The rain unexpectedly added to the atmosphere, making the discussion feel calm and personal. What I enjoyed most was seeing how a simple activity could bring people closer and remind us of Gods presence in our interactions.

   This experience made me realize that Gods care shows up not only in perfect moments but also in times of change and uncertainty. I learned how important it is to stay open and positive when plans dont go as expected. The activity helped me see the beauty of fellowship and how sharing faith with others can strengthen our own. If I could do it again, I would make an effort to talk to more people and listen to their insights, since everyones experience offers something valuable.

   Because of this activity, I am more aware of how God works in both simple and unexpected ways. I am more open to forming meaningful relationships and seeing Gods love through others. Most importantly, I am reminded that even when things dont go as planned, Gods care remains constant and unchanging.
