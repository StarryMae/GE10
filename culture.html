ASIN: Hining ng Lahi

   On Friday, September 19, 2025, from 7:00 PM to 9:00 PM, I attended a musical performance at the Luce Auditorium in Silliman University. The event featured the famous Filipino band ASIN, known for their traditional and folk-inspired music. The goal of the session was to let the audience experience old Filipino music and appreciate its cultural roots. It was fascinating to hear instruments and melodies that felt both nostalgic and refreshing.

   I did not think I would to enjoy the performance as much as I did. I thought old-style would not really interest me, but hearing ASIN perform live change my perspective. Their songs carried deep meanings about life, love, and the environment, and it made me realize how powerful music can be in telling stories and expressing emotions. I was so amazed by the instrument they used, it was quite unique and were ones I had never seen before.

   It reminded me that even though time changes trends, old music still has a strong influence. I learned that preserving and appreciating traditional art forms is important because they tell the stories of who we are as a people. It was really nostalgic and magical hearing them perform such wonderful music.

   Because of this activity, I am more appreciative of Filipino culture and heritage. I learned to listen with an open mind and to value the beauty in older forms of art. It made me proud to see how music connects generations and continues to inspire people today.

Yuko Takanashi Dance Company: The Unseen Flame

   On Saturday, September 27, 2025, from 7:00 Pm to 9:00 PM, I attended a performance at the Silliman University Luce Auditorium. The show was called The Unseen Flame by the Yuko Takahashi Dance Company, and it was a modern ballet about the tragic history of Japan. I went there as an attendee, and I honestly did not know what to expect at first. I thought it would just be a typical dance show, but it turned out to be something deeper and really emotional.

   Watching the performance was a unique experience. The movements of the dancers and the music felt powerful, even without any words. It was interesting how they were able to tell a story about Japans past just through dance. I also noticed how graceful yet intense the dancers were, it made me realize how much effort and emotion go into performing. One thing that surprised me was how I could actually feel the sadness and strength in their movements. It made me appreciate how art can express things that are sometimes hard to say.

   This experience really opened my eyes to how dance and art can tell stories and preserve culture. I learned that you do not need to fully understand everything to be moved by it. I am glad I got to see it because it made me appreciate art in a new way.

Pasipo Vocal Ensemble: The Art of Opera

   On Saturday, October 18, 2025, from 7:00 PM to 9:00 PM, I attended The Art of Opera performed by the Pasipo Vocal Ensemble at the Luce Auditorium in Silliman University. I went there as an attendee, simply curious about what opera would be like since I have mostly only heard about it but never actually experienced it live. So I bought my own tickets just to see how it was like.

   I thought opera would be hard to appreciate because it is in a different language and has such a formal reputation. But when the performance started, I was amazed by how powerful and emotional the singers voices were. Even without understanding every word, I could still feel the emotions through their tone, expression, and movements. I was surprised by how passionate and dramatic it all was, it felt like a mix of theater and music, but on a much deeper level. I was so amazed by how they could control their own voices in such a high note for a long period of time.

   I learned that music does not really need a translation. It is not something old or boring, it is actually quite beautiful. Buying those tickets were worth it in my opinion. But next time I would research more about the pieces beforehand.
